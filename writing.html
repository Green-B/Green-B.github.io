Everyday Physics, and Your Own Particle Accelerator

Most of the public’s exposure to physics comes from either learning Newton’s laws in high school or news of massive experiments like the Large Hadron Collider, so most modern physics research goes under the radar. Thus, when a friend offered me the chance to write a guest column for the local newspaper, I opted to write about a few of the most active subfields in physics and their relevance to the technologies we rely on today.

On Empathy

As an undergraduate, I survived a brain tumor. The disease and the treatment were invisible from the outside, and my family and I contemplated at that time how many others must likewise have undetectable trials and tribulations. We have tried to consider this in our interactions going forward. As a graduate student I put these thoughts to word in and then adapted them for a radio segment.
